How to Perform the Daily Examen

  1. Become aware of God's presence. Look back on the events of the day in the company of the Holy Spirit.
  2. Review the day with gratitude. Walk through your day in the presence of God and note its joys and delights.
  3. Pay attention to your emotions. Reflect on the feelings you experienced during the day.
  4. Choose one feature of the day and pray from it. Ask the Holy Spirit to direct you to something during the day that God thinks is particularly important.
  5. Look toward tomorrow. Ask God to give you light for tomorrow's challenges.

History of the Examen

1491-1556

St. Ignatius of Loyola, founder of the Jesuits, develops the Spiritual Exercises, including the Examen.

16th Century

The Examen becomes a core practice in Jesuit spirituality, spreading throughout the Catholic world.

20th Century

Renewed interest in Ignatian spirituality leads to wider adoption of the Examen beyond Catholic circles.

21st Century

The Examen gains popularity as a mindfulness practice, adapted for various spiritual and secular contexts.
